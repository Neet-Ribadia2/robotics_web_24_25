What's BEST

BEST is a national eight-week robotics competition in the United States, held each fall. It is designed to inspire middle school and high school students to explore engineering careers by building robots, solving real-world problems, and developing teamwork skills.

BEST History

The BEST competition began in 1993 when Texas Instruments engineers Ted Mahler and Steve Marum, inspired by a video of MIT freshmen building a robot, created the initiative during an Engineering Day event in Sherman. engaging students in engineering, science, and technology.

How can students join the team, and what are the expectations?


Students can join the robotics team by attending club meetings every day and selecting one of the three divisions: marketing, programming, or construction. Members are expected to actively participate in their chosen division, collaborate with teammates, and contribute to projects. Regular attendance and effective communication are important to ensure everyone can work together towards our goals and make the most out of the experience!

What competitions do we participate in, and how do we prepare for them?

We participate in BEST Robotics competitions, where our focus is on completing specific tasks rather than traditional preparation methods. Throughout the season, members are assigned various tasks related to their division—marketing, programming, or construction—that contribute to our overall project. This approach helps us stay organized and ensures that everyone plays a vital role in our competition efforts.

Do we welcome beginners, and how can one improve their skills and knowledge?

Yes, we absolutely welcome beginners! We provide resources for skill development, including hands-on instruction on using tools for construction and introductory lessons on programming to help new members get started. Our experienced members are always ready to support and guide beginners as they learn and grow within the club

What types of projects do we typically work on, and how are they selected?

We typically work on a variety of projects that are assigned based on the annual theme for BEST Robotics. These themes are designed to be relevant and applicable for schools, allowing us to create projects that can be utilized in educational settings. Since the themes are predetermined, our focus is on effectively completing the assigned projects rather than selecting them ourselves.
